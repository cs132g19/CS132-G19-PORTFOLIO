Hi! We are CANDOR!

Our project, Tweet Analysis on False Coronavirus Home Remedies, aims to utilize data science to understand the public perception and beliefs of Philippine Citizens in regards to supposed methods to prevent or cure the SARS-CoV-2 virus. Using data obtained from Twitter and tools such as Python and its libraries, our goal is to provide an analysis that can give insight to the spread of covid-related misinformation.

Here's an overview of our project.

Misinformation has always been a huge problem when it comes to social media, particularly because people tend to accept what they read for what it is, without choosing to check if the information shared is correct. Because of the panic during the early months of the coronavirus pandemic, many people have been desperate for remedies and prevention techniques that they can do from home without spending excessive amounts of money. This eventually lead to them sharing the rumored techniques, secrets and discoveries that can supposedly help which further led to mass sharing of such misinformation.

We chose to analyze this topic specifically because the pandemic has impacted all of our lives, changing it drastically. It further brought to light how many people can be easily deceived or given false hope via misinformation. Through this project, our goal is to find out when was the spread of fake home remedies and techniques more likely to happen and what factors are involved.

Research Question: When did tweets about Covid home remedies/prevention techniques become more likely to go viral in relation to the Covid-19 pandemic in the Philippines?

Hypothesis

Tweets about Covid home remedies/preventions are most likely to go viral during the first months of the pandemic in the country and less likely as time goes on.

Null Hypothesis

Tweets about Covid home remedies/preventions are likely to go viral anytime during the pandemic (first case to 2022).

Action Plan

Analyze the engagements and/or frequency of tweets about Covid home remedies or preventions and correlate it with the date it was posted in relation to the beginning of the pandemic in the country (eg. post date - date of first case).
