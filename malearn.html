Statistical Test

Linear Regression

Statistical Tests help us understand the data we collect and make inferences. Typically, they are used to examine relationships between variables and test hypotheses. In other words, they are a way of analyzing dadta to see if there is a significant difference between groups. Linear Regression, our chosen statistical test, helps us find a relationship between the predictors and a response variable. What we are trying to do is to create a line of best fit. Naturally, since this a linear regression, we are attempting to find a linear relationship.

Machine Learning Model

Event Detection

Event Detection is a type of machine learning model that helps in identifying trends and events. For our study, we aim to identify events that have caused peaks in our data, specifically what causes misinformation about COVID-19 home remedies and techniques to rise.

Our event detection model uses tweet count and date as variables. The dotted lines represent the change in trend, in which two occur on June, 5, 2020 and February 28, 2022. The red cross symbols represent the peaks in our data. Here are the events based on the model:

March 2020: President Duterte imposes the first nationwide lockdown shortly after the first local transmission in the Philippines.

March 2021: Cases finally reach the one million mark, and the first batches of the vaccines roll in.

August 2021: The Delta variant of COVID-19 has become the dominant coronavirus in the Philippines.

January 2022: President Duterte imposes the first nationwide lockdown shortly after the first local transmission in the Philippines.

In short, these events caused a change in the number of misinformation tweets being posted. The first dotted line shows a change in trend with the sudden drop of tweets after a few months into the pandemic, whereas the second dotted line represents the change in trend after around a year into the pandemic and when more minor events and circumstances such as vaccine drives and case counts.
